      • Hollywood Kitchen
        $$ Diners
        6801 Hollywood Blvd.
        Los Angeles, CA 90028
        11/7/2015
        1 check-in First to Review

        Hollywood Kitchen takes over the 4th level space at the Hollywood and Highland Center formerly occupied by Wood Fire BBQ.  The new restaurant about 2 weeks old according to the waitress specializes in cold and hot sandwiches and burgers served up with their crispy fresh cut fries.

        Although I had already eaten lunch at PokiNometry located on the same level but at the opposite end of the mall a mere hour beforehand I couldn't pass up the opportunity to try out a new restaurant and possibly land an FTR.

        Perusing the menu which is brought to your table on a clipboard by the friendly waitress I settled on the Farm Boy, a hot sandwich which combines grilled chicken, bacon, provolone cheese, lettuce and tomato topped off by a perfectly fried egg with a runny yolk and served on a soft brioche bun.  The grilled chicken is actually a meaty dark meat chicken thigh which was not an issue for me as I prefer dark meat over white meat anyway.

        As I sat and waited for my sandwich to be prepared I noticed the restaurant's interior remains fairly unchanged from when Wood Fire BBQ occupied the space.  Square wooden tables for 2 and 4 occupy much of the space.  A communal table which seats from 10 to 14 sits in the exact spot I remembered it at the previous business as does three wooden booths with vinyl bench seating.

        Pop music but performed in a Middle Eastern language plays overhead but at a very comfortable volume.

        Since I was the only diner at the time my sandwich was plated and brought out to me in just a matter of minutes.  As you'll see on my photo the sandwich looked pretty filling.

        The ingredients all looked and tasted fresh.  I pushed down on the sandwich top just a bit and unfortunately popped the egg yolk which ran down the sides of the sandwich creating a gooey but delicious mess.  The fresh cut fries looked a tad oily when brought out on the platter but tastewise it was fine, soft in its interior but crispy outside.  Probably because the frying oil was clean it did not taste oily at all.

        I managed to clean off my plate and left the restaurant overly stuffed thanks to two lunches within two hours.

        Open for lunch and dinner their hours are still in experimental phase.  Credit cards accepted they do both eat-in and takeout options.

      • Mahalo!
        $ Breakfast & Brunch, Hawaiian, Burgers
        1376 9th Ave
        San Francisco, CA 94122
        7/18/2015
        ROTD 11/6/2015
        Listed in Rotterdam!

        Mahalo takes over the long-time business site for M's Cafe.   I just stumbled upon it while looking for a place to grab some grub this afternoon.   The big menu posted on the front window caught my eye instantly, and I knew right away I wanted to try them out.  Many friends who follow my reviews know that I love Hawaiian plate lunches.

        The menu is huge featuring Hawaiian items, breakfast items, burgers & sandwiches.  I've scanned their take out copy and posted it in the photo section.  I ordered the House Chicken Wings, a BBQ Mix Plate and a Spam Musubi.  I also had a cold beverage to wash it all down with.

        As soon as I sat down I knew this would be a different Hawaiian lunch plate experience as a real metal fork and an actual steak knife with teeth was placed in front of me.  There's no dining with disposable products here.  The food is served on actual plates; although if you miss the plastic utensils and recyclable containers you can always order for take out.

        For their plate lunches they offer two sizes like most Hawaiian places.  The standard of 2 scoops of steamed white rice and 1 scoop of macaroni salad is offered even though you can substitute brown rice and/or a green salad at an additional cost.

        The service was very good.  I'm not the kind of diner who asks a lot of questions and the few that I did ask were answered promptly and with a smile.

        The Musubi (yelp.com/biz_photos/maha…) arrived first.  It was a bit rice heavy and I wished the slice of Spam were a bit thicker, but that seems to be the trend at other places I frequent so I accept that.  It tasted like what I'm familiar with and I gladly devoured it in 5 bites.

        Next up was the BBQ Mix Plate (yelp.com/biz_photos/maha…) with 1 strip of beef short rib, 2 pieces of grilled chicken thigh and 2 pieces of tender teriyaki beef.  Being the Chinese guy that I am I dove into the rice first and it was cooked right.  The proteins were all cooked as expected with no extra char, no lingering aftertastes and no extra salt.  Success!  I was actually pretty full already, but I did have 1 other item to dig into.

        The House Chicken Wings (yelp.com/biz_photos/maha…) arrived as I was doing damage to the Mix Plate.  These are "big boy" wings 4 inches in length and plump, NOT the little mini ones you get at KFC or many wing-specific eateries.  The exterior was nicely seasoned and had a terrific crunch while the inside meat was moist and also had seasoning.

        Open from 9 AM to 9 PM I can see myself popping in from time to time trying some breakfast fare and other plate lunches.  Credit cards are accepted and they offer delivery to a specific area with a $20+ minimum order.

      • Sonoma Cider
        Beer, Wine & Spirits
        30C Mill St
        Healdsburg, CA 95448
        5/27/2015
        1 check-in
        Listed in Adventures in Alcohol

        I really didn't know much about hard ciders except that most are derived from fruit at some stage so when the opportunity came up to take a tour of a few Northern California cider producers with a cider expert I took advantage of it.

        Sonoma Cider located in downtown Healdsburg was our first stop.  I don't get out to Healdsburg much but among my few visits there was a session where I first learned how to bake from scratch.  Coincidentally it had to do with creating a pastry dough and utilizing something well known in this region the Gravenstein apple.  Hopefully, I'll remember to review that adventure in the near future.

        Although we arrived a bit late for our scheduled tour we were happily greeted and welcomed by Robert Cordtz, one of the founders of Sonoma Cider and several members of his staff in their tasting room lobby.  After a quick introduction we got right into the cider tasting phase as the crew at Sonoma Cider was eager for us to try their product.  With each cider poured we learned a little more about what we were drinking and how they differ from one another both on taste and production, and we got to learn about the history of Sonoma Cider and how it all began in late 2013 with Sonoma's own Gravenstein apple.

        Inside the tasting room all of the ciders are iced down and tapped like a fine craft beer.  We started out with pours of The Hatchet, their signature apple cider and the Golden Delicious, an exclusive to the store.  Equally tasty both samples had a beer-like subtlety yet with fruity notes.  I found the taste very likeable, different for me and refreshing. We also tried The Pitchfork which adds pear juice into the blend, The Anvil which has a home-brewed bourbon infused into it and their limited edition The Washboard infused with sarsaparilla root from India and vanilla. We were all asked to pick a favorite for which we were poured a generous pint to enjoy while touring the production facility.  I chose The Anvil.

        They use only organic apples from Washington and of course Sonoma's own Gravenstein.  In order to have fresh apple juice throughout the year their apples are put into cold storage, and processed by a third party who delivers the pressed juice in tanker trucks one day after the order is put in.

        After running through various tests for sweetness and acidity the juice is heated, mixed with yeast to start the fermentation process then transferred into gigantic temperature-controlled tanks for two weeks.  During fermentation the sweetness of the juice disappears and is turned into alcohol.  We were given little shots so we could taste the product during the different stages of fermentation.  The end product is like a dry white wine.  The juice then travels by hose into 2000 liter blending tanks where they are married with fresh juices and delightful flavors.  Once everything is blended the juice goes directly into the giant Carbo-Cooler which has liquid glycol and CO2 piped into it.  In just a few minutes the cider is cooled and gets its carbonation.  We were informed that their Carbo-Cooler purchased from an old Dr. Pepper plant was recently featured on an episode of Unwrapped which airs on one of the cooking channels.

        The finished product goes directly to the bottling process.  When running at full speed the automated bottling machine which has 34 filling valves and 8 cappers processes about 250 bottles per minute.  After inspection they then travel to the labeling and boxing stages.

        One of the fastest growing cider companies in the U.S. less than 2 years into the business the production numbers have jumped from 70,000 cases in 2014 to a projection of 150,000 cases by the end of 2015.  Their certified organic cider is available throughout the U.S., in Canada and in China.

        This was an amazing tour and seeing how most of the group made purchases after the tour I think they gained several new fans for their product.  I've included photos to describe my tour better.

      • Tonli Dumpling House
        $$ Food Trucks
        2122 N Main St
        Walnut Creek, CA 94596
        5/26/2015
        Listed in My Asian Noodle Fix, A Seafood Odyssey, Street Food Frenzy

        My review is for the food truck which we spotted at the SOMA StrEat Food Park one Saturday afternoon.  We were holding a fundraiser for one of the non-profits that I volunteer for, and naturally I volunteered to help out.  Part of what I was instructed to do was to situate myself at the main entrance of the food park and offer up information on who we were, what we do and what we were trying to achieve at the fundraiser.

        Since the food park had just opened and the lunch rush had not started yet the cool owner of the Tonli Dumpling House food truck walked over and struck up a nice conversation to gather more information about our organization.  Since we share a love for eating good food our conversation soon turned into one about food and eventually a discussion about the food that he takes pride in preparing on his food truck.

        Not being one who goes out looking for food trucks I had never heard of Tonli Dumpling House so when the owner asked that we stop by and give his food a try and offered a generous discount to our volunteers as part of his contribution to our organization I spread the word to our volunteers, and after my shift was over I took him up on his offer.  A couple of ladies on our volunteer team had already purchased food at the food truck, and told me whatever they had ordered they loved.

        By the time I visited the menu selections for the truck (yelp.com/biz_photos/tonl…) were down to 6 choices and 2 of them caught my eye so I ordered both.  As promised I purchased the food at a discount as several of our volunteers had before me.

        My food was ready in a short time, and I couldn't wait to dig in.  The food park was pretty packed at the time but luckily our organization roped off a table for the volunteers to use.

        The Crispy Fried Crab Dumplings (yelp.com/biz_photos/tonl…) had a nice pocket of fresh crab inside the deep fried wonton wrapper, and it was properly seasoned which meant I didn't have to put to use the very generic sweet and sour dipping sauce.

        The Braised Pork Belly Ramen (yelp.com/biz_photos/tonl…) really hit the spot with a deceptive amount of noodles which I thought was a lot less than there was and a generous portion of rich, tender and super flavorful pork belly.  Yup, melt-in-your-mouth pork belly goodness!  The broth was just a tad on the salty side, but after eating all of the firm noodles, the pork belly and the near-perfect egg half I was okay with not finishing the broth.

        All in all, 4 stars for the food but I threw in the extra 5th star as a thank you to the food truck owner for his generosity towards our organization and our volunteers.

      • Taqueria Dos Amigos
        $ Mexican
        850 Brannan St
        San Francisco, CA 94103
        5/6/2015 Updated review
        10 check-ins
        Listed in Frijoles Negros Sólo Por Favor!, A Seafood Odyssey, Give Me Justice & Give Me Eats!, Taco The Town

        I think I've probably eaten here often enough to warrant an update on my review from way back when.

        There are still much better places for burritos nearby, but Taqueria Dos Amigos is about a block away from where I work, and for convenience sake especially on a busy work day I don't mind coming here to grab a quick burrito lunch.

        Their burritos are definitely on the small side so I'm definitely forced into having smaller portions which isn't necessarily a bad thing especially for an old guy like me.  There is not much difference in size for a regular burrito as opposed to a super burrito.  Sure, they give you more ingredients in the super but you seemingly get less of each ingredient so the burrito comes out to be the same size yet you are paying more.

        I order the same things with each visit.  My usual is a regular burrito with the tomatillo chicken which I just refer to as "green chicken", black beans and spicy salsa.  I like the green chicken.  It's all chicken breast meat and often in huge chunks but it has good flavor in it and it's fairly moist.  For a change of pace I'll order the prawn burrito, but that just means having to wait longer as the prawns are cooked to order.

        Their carnitas are decent, but I've never taken a liking to any of their preparations of beef as I've found their beef to be too dry, too bland, too grisly or a combination of all of that.

        The staff is very nice too.  So a 3 it is!

      • Aloha BBQ
        $ Hawaiian, Chinese, Barbeque
        4935 Mission St
        San Francisco, CA 94112
        4/25/2015
        3 check-ins First to Review
        Listed in One Scoop Macaroni, Two Scoops Rice, Wing Things

        That was short lived!  After eating here a second time I'm dropping my rating to a 3.

        This is a new Hawaiian BBQ restaurant located directly across the street from the Safeway supermarket near Geneva and Mission Streets.  I was just walking by on my way to run some errands and was actually thinking about Hawaiian plate lunch but at another establishment further down the street.

        The menu here is much larger than my intended lunch spot with a wide assortment of Hawaiian BBQ lunch plates, Chinese and Hawaiian appetizers,  musubi, barbecue bowls, burgers, saimin noodles or chow mein and more.  In fact, it took me awhile to read through the menu and decide what I wanted to try.

        For my first order here I had the Hawaiian BBQ Beef (yelp.com/biz_photos/aloh…) plate.  It is the standard combination of sweet marinated beef on top of a pile of shredded cabbage with two scoops of steamed white rice and a scoop of macaroni salad.  It was quite good and very filling with the beef being leaner than most that I have had at other Hawaiian BBQ restaurants.

        My second visit began very annoyingly as the cash register attendant obviously did not understand his job very well.  He did not know what was on the menu and how to input the items into the cash register repeatedly having to run to the back of the restaurant for assistance.  I ordered 2 items and a soda and it took almost 10 minutes.  The food delivery remained very fast however.

        Their Chef's Curry Chicken Wings (yelp.com/biz_photos/aloh…) had a faint scent of curry and absolutely zero curry flavor.  The wings were not plucked clean and chicken hairs were highly visible on several of the wings.  The exterior was hot and crispy but the wing had very little flavor.

        I also ordered the Loco Moco (yelp.com/biz_photos/aloh…).  Here they ask you how you'd like your eggs prepared, but since the worker couldn't figure out the cash register system I ended up ordering my eggs over medium.  The burgers are not hand formed like my usual Hawaiian place but more like the frozen beef patties you'd find in a supermarket freezer.  The gravy was very salty.

        They have a big refrigerator full of sodas, water or juice.  You order at the counter and your meal is ready in less than 10 minutes.  Everything is served in compostable take-out containers with non compostable forks and knives.

        There is plenty of seating for up to 49 persons.  There is currently one TV with a mounting rack next to it that I'm assuming will have another TV attached to it soon.

        Open daily 9:30 AM to 10:00 PM.  Credit cards are accepted, but there is a $3 minimum.  They do catering and they have delivery with a $20 minimum.

      • Queen’s Beauty House
        $ Hair Salons
        1522 Geneva Ave
        San Francisco, CA 94112
        2/14/2015
        2 check-ins First to Review

        As of February 7, 2015 Queen's Beauty House has relocated to this new location across the street from Crocker Amazon Playground about five blocks from their old location.  I showed up at the old location earlier today only to find signage  informing the public that they had moved.  I arrived on foot and since it was already nearly 5:30 PM and I was certain they close at 6:00 PM I had to hustle up the hill to make sure I would be able to get in for my mandatory pre-Chinese New Year haircut.

        Slightly out of breath and hair drenched in sweat the staff welcomed me into their new shop.  They knew right away I wanted a haircut.  Although they take appointments I rarely make them and I usually just walk in and hope someone is available to give me a cut.  Someone always is.

        Queen's has been my regular spot for a haircut for the past four years mainly because their location is easily accessible for me, near my home and even within walking distance if it's a nice day for 2 mile walk.  These days as my hair growth gets thinner and thinner my haircuts are pretty basic and seemingly very little work for the salon workers.  On average I'd say I'm in and out in less than 10 minutes, and as long as some hair is left on my head so that I'm not completely bald I generally leave in a happy mood knowing I won't need to pick up a comb for another two months.

        The cost for a haircut is $12.  That's very affordable by my standards and since I average one haircut every 3 months it's really no big deal budget-wise.  Queen's does a very good job and they've earned my trust.

        Queen's offers salon services for women too, but you'll have to read it from a woman reviewer to find out how that goes.

      • Elite Event: Black & Gold Brunch
        Yelp Events
        280 Valencia St
        San Francisco, CA 94103
        1/19/2015
        1 check-in
        Listed in Yelptastic!

        Hosted by Chef/Owner Alexander Alioto and his amazing staff at Plin this past Sunday Yelp and community manager Abby S brought together a small group of Yelpers with either a black or a gold Yelp Elite badge to celebrate 2015 in style. This was easily the smallest and one of the most intimate Yelp events I've ever attended, and I had a great time.

        I arrived a bit early as things were still being set up and at Abby's suggestion I took a seat at the bar where I ordered a Drake's Imperial Stout (yelp.com/biz_photos/elit…).  This was my first experience with this brew and I loved it with its slight chocolate hues and its smoothness.  The football game was being shown on the television, but I couldn't really concentrate on the game as a keyboardist played in the background and a young lady whose name escapes me as I write this review (Sorry!  I'm old!) serenaded us with her amazing mezzo-soprano vocals.

        As more Yelpers begun to arrive we were invited to look for our name tags and have a seat at the table.  Although we were told we could sit in any seat Abby had seated us at random at a long table set for 18.  Great!  I love meeting other Yelpers whom I've never met or haven't seen in awhile.

        At each setting we got a preview of the menu (yelp.com/biz_photos/elit…) and gifts from Team Yelp (yelp.com/biz_photos/elit…).  Very cool!  I wasn't expecting a metal flask with a big Yelp logo emblazoned on it, but I love it.  It will get used!

        After a round of brief introductions the food was on.  Served family style the Tuna Poke (yelp.com/biz_photos/elit…) and the Handmade Doughnuts (yelp.com/biz_photos/elit…) arrived first.  All I could say is yum and yummier!  I love poke as long as it's fresh and this definitely was.  And who can say no to light and airy donuts rolled in sugar and dunked and drizzled in chocolate sauce?  I couldn't.

        The Chopped Salad (yelp.com/biz_photos/elit…) arrived next.  I'm not fond of blue cheese but liked that this salad had some candied hazelnut in there.

        Then the first wow dish arrived.  Chef Alioto's famous Raviolo al Uovo (yelp.com/biz_photos/elit…).  I remembered reading about this in a review posted by Yelp favorite Cherylynn N and this was all of the deliciousness I remembered her describing with its runny egg yolk all wrapped up nicely in a perfectly cooked pasta shell.

        The wait staff brought us some airy, warm French bread for which I used to soak up all of that delicious egg yolk and brown butter goodness on my plate.

        We weren't done yet though.  The Baked French Toast with Mascarpone Cheese Quenelle (yelp.com/biz_photos/elit…) came out sizzling in little stone boats.  These were perfectly executed big chunks of bread dunked (thankfully not soaked) in egg batter and covered with walnut syrup and roasted pecans.  I enjoyed this as well.

        Our final dish was the Crispy Polenta Cakes with Braised Oxtail (yelp.com/biz_photos/elit…).  I was pretty full by the time this arrived but I enjoyed it nonetheless.  The polenta was nice and firm as was the poached egg.  Shreds of tender, flavorful braised oxtail finished this dish.

        In addition to serving up some fantastic dishes the amazing waitstaff kept our water glasses filled and brought us mimosas, Bloody Marys and sparkling wines continuously throughout our meal.

        Thank you to Chef Alioto and his stellar staff at Plin, Yelp and Abby S for putting all of this together.  It was indeed an amazing Sunday to remember!

      • Dinosaurs Vietnamese Sandwiches
        $ Vietnamese, Sandwiches
        18 Boardman Pl
        San Francisco, CA 94103
        1/10/2015
        65 check-ins
        Listed in Asian Dessert Cart, Some Call Them Sammies, Give Me Justice & Give Me Eats!, But Don't Call Me Chilly Willy!

        When it comes to eating Vietnamese sandwiches I'm generally pretty easy to please.  As long as the bread is good and the components are fresh I'll have very few complaints.  That said I was quite thrilled when I found out through Yelp of course that Dinosaurs had opened their 4th shop less than two blocks away from my work.

        I've long been a fan of Dinosaurs and I frequented their original location in the Castro quite a bit from 2010 when they opened until 2012; however, subsequent changes to my work schedule have limited my visits since.  The meatball sandwich had been my favorite of their sandwiches.  I don't know what it is about ground meat formed into balls but no matter the type of cuisine I love meatballs.

        Not having been to any Dinosaurs location in over a year I was excited to see a larger menu than I remembered featuring 2 types of salads, 3 types of spring rolls, 8 types of sandwiches and 5 specialty drinks.  

        On my first visit I had, of course, the Meatball sandwich (yelp.com/biz_photos/dino…).  The French baguette they use here was toasted perfectly exhibiting a nice exterior crunch and the softness of fresh bread on the inside.  Piled high with pickled carrot and daikon, slices of crisp jalapenos, fresh cilantro and a crisp cucumber spear with just a smear of mayo and ground pepper the meatballs were every bit as tasty as I remembered with just the right amount of firmness to hold it all together.

        One Vietnamese sandwich rarely fills me up, and at most places I go it takes about two sandwiches to do the job.  So far on my many visits to this Dinosaurs I've found that one and a half would be perfect for my midday appetite.

        In addition to the Meatball sandwich I've also ordered:

        The Special (yelp.com/biz_photos/dino…) with three types of pork products piled high.
        Shaking Beef (yelp.com/biz_photos/dino…) with its super tasty beef sauteed with chopped onions.
        Grilled Chicken (yelp.com/biz_photos/dino…) with a lemongrass infused chicken thigh.

        The Roasted Portobello (yelp.com/biz_photos/dino…) comes with veganaise instead of mayo, but a check of the ingredients found that it was soy based.  That's something I can't have in my diet so the kind folks here have omitted it and given me the regular mayo at my request.  I'm a big fan of mushrooms so it's no surprise that this is probably my second favorite sandwich here.

        In an area of the City where good eats is very limited I'm so happy to have Dinosaurs 4 so close by.

      • Wood Fire BBQ - CLOSED
        $$ Barbeque, Southern
        6801 Hollywood Blvd
        Los Angeles, CA 90028
        1/6/2015
        1 check-in
        Listed in Fire Up The Grill!

        Ever have one of those days when your schedule is thrown completely off and it's past 9 PM, you're not in your home city, you haven't had dinner yet and more than half of the nearby restaurants have already closed on a weeknight?

        Well if you haven't figured it out yet that's what brought me to Wood Fire BBQ recently.  Wood Fire BBQ is located inside the Hollywood Highland Mall.  The hotel I was staying at was a mere three blocks away, and I recalled having a fairly good BBQ sandwich here about a year ago.

        The restaurant was still very busy with lots of tables filled both inside and out.  I was greeted at the front of the restaurant and promptly seated in a large, comfortable booth inside looking directly at one of the prep stations.  My waitress came by, greeted me, introduced herself, handed me a menu and placed a set of utensils in front of me.  I ordered a soft drink and a glass of water right away.  Don't think it's weird when you see the same gentleman who buses the tables, grab clean glasses from another area and bring you your drinks.  As I remembered I believe this was the same gentleman who served me my drinks a year ago.  That's just the way they do things here.

        I was hungry having not eaten in about 8 hours so I decided to order something heavy.  I didn't have to be in the office until 10:30 the next morning so I knew I had plenty of time to let my food digest.  I went with the 2 Meat Combo and chose the BBQ Chicken Half and the Baby Back Ribs as my meat choices and Garlic Mashed Potatoes and Corn on the Cob as my sides.  Looking back I probably should not have ordered the corn since this was already out of season and what I was served was probably pulled out of the freezer.  The combo also came with cornbread.

        The wait for my food was rather long.  Considering that most of the items I ordered were probably already cooked and just needed heating up I couldn't understand why it took so long.  Seeing some of the staff standing around playing with their phones and chit-chatting with each other didn't set off a good impression either.

        After maybe a 25 minute wait one of the servers brought me my food (yelp.com/biz_photos/wood…).  I dug in immediately starting with the cornbread.  It was dry and tasteless.  Definitely needed a gulp of liquid to wash it down.  The mashed potatoes were very good and the garlic in there was very prominent.  I enjoyed that.  The chicken half was huge.  I started with the chicken drumstick and thigh and both were moist and quite good.  The breast meat however was definitely on the dry side.  I might note though that the chicken would have been a bit under-seasoned if not for the sweet BBQ sauce on there.

        My ribs were super meaty, but they gave me the end area with all the soft bones and thick globs of fat.  Flavorful mostly from the sweet sauce and super meaty the pork was tender but definitely not fall-off-the-bone tender.  I managed to pick off most of the meat which was a lot.

        The corn on the cob is served Mexican style slathered with a mayo-like sauce and dusted in cheese.  It was still hot when they served this.  The kernels on the corn were huge and unfortunately very tough.  As much as I enjoyed the serving style I couldn't finish even half of the corn for it would have been an incredible workout for my teeth.

        My waitress came by not once, but twice to check up on how things were and even refilled my drinks twice.  All in all, the food filled me up which was exactly what I wanted.  It's not the best BBQ I've had but I'm glad they were there and still open when most of the other restaurants were not.
