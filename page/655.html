      • DASH Japanese Tapas & Sushi
        $$ Japanese, Sushi Bars, Tapas Bars
        220 Main St
        San Mateo, CA 94401
        7/16/2014 Updated review
        Purchased a Yelp Deal 1 check-in
        Listed in Sushi Hits & Misses

        Definitely a solid 4.

        However, like my last visit, some things were hit or miss.

        This time I got the uni/oyster app topped with quail egg (+$1 per 2 oysters) but the uni was old (slimy not creamy) and lacked in flavor.

        Omakase was great and included:
        - Ama ebi (plump, sweet & fresh)
        - Walu (oily and buttery as it should be)
        - Shima aji (Very fresh and great quality with a bit of grated ginger it was perfect!)
        - Maguro (Firm, crispy and sweet)
        - Hamachi toro (Not very fatty. Tasted more like kanpachi but still very fresh)
        - King salmon toro (Slight seared aburi style. I wish they hadn't seared it but again, fresh, extremely fatty and delish)
        - House cured ikura (I reallllllly like their ikura. Salty, sweet and definitely fresh. You won't see any wrinkly balls here! That's what she said.)
        - Some sort of tai? Not very memorable so must've not been good.

        Hokkaido scallops: Sooo good. Even the scallop hating fiance enjoyed them. They were every so delicately seared and had a nice smokiness to them while still being raw and juicy.

        Hirame: Topped with a drip of ponzu and grated daikon the fish was very crisp and fresh.

        Sake toro: Topped with a tart ume sauce these were a perfect balance of sweet and sour.

        Ika geso: Extremely oily and dry this time around. :(

        Side note: Free small sake with check in. Woohoo!

        Ok I'm going to be one of those douche bags ("Well I lived in Japan for 10 days" puahahaaa) but no… Ok I'm going to be one of those douche bags ("Well I lived in Japan for 10 days" puahahaaa) but no really. I lived in Tokyo for the past 3 months so I've been spoiled by fresh fish. Imagine eating a box of Hokkaido uni from Tsukiji EVERY SINGLE DAY. Yeah that was me! But after moving back to the bay I refused to eat sushi, god I'm a douche, until the craving hit and I had to cave. I went to Dash with some friends for a birthday and ordered damn near everything they had. Let me tell you, they did not disappoint (most of the time)! They even had special stuff like ao ebi (blue shrimp). Anyways... onto the food.

        Omakase sashimi platter:
        The one we got has chu toro, tai, kanpachi, hotaru ika, sake toro, ao ebi, walu (I think). Chu toro, kanpachi, sake toro, walu were on pooooint. So fresh.

        Hotaru ika: Weren't fresh tasting. Not as sweet as they should be. Nothing beats having this fresh while in Japan. =P

        Anago: I loooove anago. Just not in the US apparently. Theirs was old, slighty dry (it should be buttery and tender), they topped it with some thick super sweet teriyaki-ish sauce. No bueno.

        Uni, tobiko & oysters: These are so good, definitely a must have. I'd recommend asking for a quail egg yolk to make it jizz yo panties good.

        Seared chu toro: Yesss must have especially with the fresh (REAL) wasabi on top. Quality of fish was very good but the rice was a bit too firm.

        Ankimo with the usual ponzu/daiko combo: Very good. Pretty sure they make it in-house because it didn't taste like the tubes most restaurants buy and slice.

        Wagyu carpaccio: Sauce was waaaaay too sweet. It should've just been ponzu but they made it teriyaki-ish. Meat was good quality. A squeeze of lemon/acidity would've made it better. That or no sweet sauce!

        Albacore tataki: Simple, classic, typical but good.

        Ika geso: Fresh, crispy, chewy and well battered. A must have with drinking!

        Nigiri: Hirame, hiramasa, sake, ikura, unagi, kanpachi. All were VERY good and fresh. I'm surprised the hiramasa was so good. I haven't found a realllly good hiramasa in the US until Dash! Very buttery and silky. Theirs is slightly seared and sooo good.

        Side note: The bill came out to a heft $400+ but totally worth it. You're paying for quality. So thanks Yelp for the deal! I bought one after eating here cause I will definitely be back!         Read more

        Maguro: Poor cut not a high quality akami.
        Unagi: Typicay.

        I have seem some amazing photos from friends who go here for dinner on a regular basis so I definitely will be back to give them another chance.

      • Hinoki & The Bird
        $$$$ American (New)
        10 W Century Dr
        Los Angeles, CA 90067
        6/17/2014

        One of my new favs in LA!

        Interior is cool, swanky, modern but not stuck up. Service was thorough, fast and friendly. Now onto the food!

        Fav dish of the night:
        Hamachi with avocado, blood orange puree and fava beans. I need to meet whoever thought of that combo cause I wanna give em a hug! AKA kidnap them and make them cook for me every day. Fresh buttery hamachi with tangy sweet blood orange puree, creamy rich avocado and lightly blanched sweet fava beans. Must have.

        Oysters w/pear mignonette: Perfect balance of briney, tart and sweet.

        Crispy marinated chicken: Lightly battered and juicy. Think karaage.

        Chili crab toast: Savory and well spiced. Very good. Love the paper thin tart green apple. Nice contrast to the rich, spicy crab flavor.

        Pumpkin toast: Fluffy, melt in your mouth sweet pumpkin puree with tangy goat cheese, sweet/salty miso jam and pumpkin seeds on top. Yum!

        Sweet corn soup was too sweet :( but flavors were definitely unique with the cilantro oil and crisp hijiki.

        Tuna & lemongrass salad: Not enough lemongrass flavor and the tuna was a bit chewy for me sadly.

        Lobster roll: Juicy, lightly curry flavored lobster, with just enough sauce to be moist but not overpowering. The hint of floral basil made this a must have.

        Sambal skate wing: Soooo juicy and REALLY spicy. I loved the spice.

        Apple marinated shortrib: Juicy with the perfect chew. Complex with tons of depth. Spicy, sweet, soy. Must have.

        Smoked black cod: Can't go wrong with black cod. The smoked cedar (?) gave the buttery fish a robust richness.

        Drunken duck breast: Juicy on the inside, crispy on the outside.

        The sides were all amazing. Simple but refined and well executed. Spiced okra, grilled mushrooms w/sea salt & lime, roasted yam w/creme fraiche, pickled peppers & lardon was jizz your pants good.

        Side note: Menu changes often so I will definitely be back for moreeee. Who wants to take me??
