      • Grace Street
        $$ Coffee & Tea, Desserts, Shaved Ice
        17 W 32nd St
        New York, NY 10001
        11/17/2015
        9 check-ins

        Hands down my favorite no-wifi cafe to study or hang out with friends. I love the wooden décor, high ceilings and comfortable & varied seating. I don't drink coffee at all, so I usually go for one of the teas (roasted oolong milk tea or half & half). Their boba is actually very good, though the teas are more expensive than at other bubble tea places. One of its biggest appeals, though, is the hodduk, which I've never had anywhere else - a delectable doughnut-esque pastry with sweet, hot filling. Yum!

        I've also been meaning to try their shaved ice - will update when I do.

      • Nights and Weekends
        $$ Bars, Breakfast & Brunch, Caribbean
        1 Bedford Ave
        Brooklyn, NY 11222
        11/16/2015 Updated review
        1 check-in
        Listed in the yelp 100 challenge: nyc

        Sorely disappointed by my dinner here - the boyfriend didn't want to wait 30 minutes for Five Leaves, and I've wanted to try Nights & Weekends since 2013 when I swung by for a brunch snack, so we thought this would be a good replacement. Unfortunately, either they've changed their menu entirely or their dinner menu is just really not up to par with their brunch menu. My experience here was so miserable that I lost my appetite completely and left in tears.

        We ordered the chips with salsa with guacamole ($3 for guac, so $9 total), the miso harissa chicken wings and the mahi mahi tacos. The tacos were bland and not memorable. I would definitely not get them again. The wings were pretty good in retrospect - spicy and flavorful - but I wasn't very interested in them at the time because my perspective was so discolored by my disappointment with the guacamole. When the chips, salsa & guac arrived, my boyfriend wryly commented "They must make a killing on this." There's a tiny bowl of chips, a small bowl of very mediocre salsa and a small bowl of ... wait, mashed avocado? Granted, the avocado was fresh and good, but it was completely not what I was expecting. When I see "guacamole," I think of avocado, cilantro, red onions, tomato, garlic, lime juice - you know, guac. The bf said that he had a Mexican friend who made guacamole that way - just mashed avocado and salt. He even went on wikipedia and showed me that guacamole is another word for avocado. Okay.

        When I asked the waitress (who I had great difficulty flagging down) "Is this just mashed avocado?", she gave me a confused look and said "Were you expecting something else?" Maybe she's genuinely never encountered guacamole as anything else and I'm just really ignorant, but this experience left a bad taste in my mouth. It would've been so much better if she had at least said something before we ordered it. When we left almost the entire plate of wings on the table, she didn't even ask if we wanted a box or anything.

        Overall, this experience was so upsetting that I don't think I'd ever come back - especially since Five Leaves is across the street. I'd gladly wait 2 hours to eat there than come here (which is weird since this is supposed to be a sister restaurant).

      • Ten Ren’s Tea Time
        $ Coffee & Tea
        73 Mott St
        New York, NY 10013
        11/15/2015

        One of my favorite places for bubble tea - though I'm definitely partial to it because I've been going here since high school. I was really surprised when I came here yesterday though because it looks like they've totally revamped the space - more seating, more spacious and overall a more modern feel.

        The 5 stars is for both the excellent service and the delicious, authentic bubble tea. When my boyfriend got the taro green tea, he didn't realize that it was under the "fresh seasonal" section and that the taro green tea would be well, made with fresh taro. We both made faces upon our first sips - the drink was gritty and bland (he had also asked for no sugar). I was really confused because I've never had fresh taro in my bubble tea before. He was so upset with it that he originally just wanted to leave and go to a different bubble tea place, but we ended up talking to one of the guys at the counter, who offered to give us a free powdered taro replacement if we would prefer it. Thanks to him, our Ten Ren experience was completely redeemed - the powdered taro was definitely less legit but super tasty with minimal sugar.

      • Pho Bang Restaurant
        $ Vietnamese
        157 Mott St
        New York, NY 10013
        11/15/2015

        Honestly, I can't tell the difference among pho - the only times I've had subpar pho were when the broth was way too salty. I'm generally the kind of girl who opts not to dump sriracha & hoisin sauce in my pho so I can really enjoy the broth. Pho Bang's is pretty good.

        I went to Pho Bang at 6:30pm with my family last night, and I had no trouble with seating (probably because it's off Grand St. - still really close to everything, though).

        + #1 pho - I always get the standard (which also makes for an easy comparison among pho places). I got the regular size, split it with my mom, and was still super full (then again, I had appetizers). They're pretty generous with their meat.

        + Vietnamese spring rolls - Ugh so tasty. I also feel like it's pretty comparable to all the other delicious Viet spring rolls, though.

        + Grilled pork & vermicelli appetizer - The grilled pork and little squares of vermicelli come with carrots, radish, cucumber and lettuce wraps - as Raymond H. said, they're like little tacos. I love Viet grilled pork, so this was a satisfying appetizer (and Raymond loved them).

        + mussels with coconut curry - I've never really gotten anything else besides pho, but my dad got this. I really liked the coconut curry sauce, but I thought the mussels were overcooked ... but then again, I usually eat mussels cooked lightly with wine and garlic and butter, so I'm probably not the right person to talk about these.

        Overall, I thought this was a pretty solid option for a casual pho dinner. I'd definitely come here again if I'm in the area.

      • Spicy Village
        $ Chinese
        68 Forsyth St
        New York, NY 10002
        11/15/2015
        1 check-in

        This was on a Buzzfeed list of recommended noodle places for the big chicken tray, but we ended up ordering other food instead because we were anxious that the big chicken tray would be too much for us. In retrospect, it probably wouldn't have been since we got three dishes ... but at least we had variety!

        + spicy lamb hui mei ($6) - I loved the flat hand-pulled noodles and the spicy sauce, but the lamb was pretty off. It tasted like boiled lamb and it was just not very interesting. I ate a lot of the noodles, though.

        + beef brisket soup ($6) - Beef brisket was super on point - tender, delicious morsels. The noodles were lacking in flavor, but they were still pretty good. I opted for the beef & veggie broth, and that was pretty solid.

        + soup dumplings ($6) - For 9 soup dumplings, this is a pretty good deal. That said, the soup dumplings were only a little bit above average - good, non-oily broth in the dumplings, and they did hit the spot for a soup-dumpling craving, but Shanghai Manor & Joe's Shanghai's are thinner and tastier.

        Overall, I'd definitely come back to try the big chicken tray - I think that's the only truly unique entree here. The other food was pretty standard for Chinatown fare. Seating is rather limited - we had to wait a little bit, but luckily a woman was just finishing her meal as we arrived. Don't try to come here with a big group unless you're dining during off-peak hours.

      • Try The World
        $$ Food Delivery Services
        902 Broadway
        New York, NY 10010
        11/9/2015

        I was hesitant to try Try the World because of the initial poor reviews, but I think they've ironed out all the kinks - I absolutely loved my Argentina box. Although it seems a bit pricey, $40 (or only $32.50 if you buy the whole year's subscription) is actually a fantastic deal when you take into account the quality and diversity of the products, the research that goes into compiling this amazing package and the packaging & shipping.

        Although I can't speak for the authenticity of the products because I've never been to Argentina, I thoroughly enjoyed the culinary explorations - I'll list out everything so you can get a sense of what you'll get (and what you can do with them). I don't have much experience with Argentinian food, but following the directions on the little card was great.

        + Chimichurri - This chimichurri sauce was amazing - I basted my steak with the sauce before searing, and it took the steak to a whole different level. It's flavorful and a bit spicy - a little reminiscent of Worcester, but better. It was also a really nice, easy change from the normal salt & pepper treatment. It'll definitely last me a while, too, because you don't need very much.

        + Malbec wine marinade - I unfortunately dropped my jar so I wasn't able to make as much use of it as I wanted to, but I had the opportunity to marinade some chicken (for a grilled cheese sandwich) and simmer a spicy pork ragu sauce with it. It was great - sweet and rich - and added depth to my food.

        + Butter cookies - Rich and addicting, but I actually didn't find them very sweet at all (which is exactly the way I like them). They're a bit like shortbread cookies, but significantly better.

        + Dulce de leche - Oh my goodness, so creamy and delicious and sweet. I put some on ice cream and it was incredible. I can feel my hoarding tendencies coming out - currently using it very sparingly because it's definitely a special-occasion thing.

        + Green olive paste - I paired this with the malbec marinated chicken in a grilled cheese sandwich, and it was really good. I can imagine this making great hors d'œuvres. If you like olives, this is amazing.

        + Ducle de membrillo - I've had quince jam in France before, but it was really fun to try this the Argentinian way - in a pasta frola with manchego and queso blanco. I loved the way the cheese complemented the quince spread in the pie.

        I haven't tried the teas yet, but I'm not a tea connoisseur so I won't have much to say. I'm sure they're great because everything else was fantastic.

        The timing of the boxes (every 2 months) is perfect; I don't think I'll use up everything in the next two months, and it'll be fun to use them sporadically in my cooking. I loved my box so much that I bought a year's subscription as a gift for my best friend who lives pretty far away from anything ethnic - it's definitely a super fun package to get in the mail, and I think everything is totally worth it.

      • Golden Imperial Palace
        $$ Dim Sum, Cantonese, Seafood
        618 62nd St
        Brooklyn, NY 11220
        10/20/2015
        3 check-ins

        I'm a regular of this dim sum place because it has free parking and it's not too far from my house. This last time that I went was the only blip in service that I've had, but you'll now know for next time: do NOT sit upstairs.

        We usually sit downstairs in the large, well-decorated banquet hall, but after waiting for over 20 minutes, the host asked if we wanted to sit upstairs. "It's the same food and everything," he said. Well ... yes, same food, but totally different ambiance and service. The second floor looks like the restaurant forgot to finish renovating it, and there are far fewer dim sum carts pushed around. They ended up giving us a checklist of dim sum things that we could order, but we didn't even get half the food. Thankfully, we had ordered too much and we were too full (and they only charge for what they give you), but Port L. was super annoyed that he waited forever for his dan tat. He went downstairs to grab some.

        Other than that, though, service is generally pretty good because the dim sum carts get pushed around and everything is fresh. I love the shumai, steamed rice rolls, stuffed eggplant and all beancurd-wrapped things here. They also offer warm durian rolls, yum!

      • Poutine Please
        $ Food Stands, Poutineries
        34-12 36th St
        Astoria, NY 11106
        10/20/2015

        I remember sitting with Lillian as she charted out her ambitious plans for a poutine stand and collaborating with her in the test kitchen, and it is absolutely crazy to see where it is now - from the first taste batch of overly salted, thin fries to the currently perfectly crunchy but pillowy fries. Lillian and Ten have really taken off with their laborious efforts and have succeeded in producing the perfect fry (rivaling Pommes Frites, one of my favorite places in the city - dare I say better?). The gravy and cheese curds are similarly amazing.

        I had the honor of checking out Poutine Please at their first Manhattan stand this past Sunday, and I was delighted by their newest fall flavor - pumpkin spice (+ bacon), a dessert poutine. Prices are super reasonable - $6 (+ $1 for bacon) for the portion of poutine. It's small but filling (for me, at least). I actually don't have a favorite because they're all so good, though I would definitely recommend the pumpkin spice one while it lasts because it's one of those unusual, creative combinations that actually taste better than it sounds. Service is awesome - the fries are made fresh and the gravies are kept warm, so you basically get your poutine within a few minutes. Yay Poutine Please! I can't wait til it expands and offers even more fun flavors.

      • Hudson Grille
        $$ American (New)
        165 Mamaroneck Ave
        White Plains, NY 10601
        10/20/2015
        1 check-in

        This restaurant is surprisingly very, very good. It's not so good that I'd ask my friends to come up from the city to try their food, but I'd definitely be down to come here for brunch at least once a month. I had the huevos rancheros and the chorizo was the best chorizo I've had since Los Comales in Durham, NC; I feel like I'm constantly searching for that elusive, perfect chorizo in the Northeast and I found it ... here. My boyfriend got the crab benedict; I usually am not a fan of crabcakes (even though I love crab) but these crabcakes were seriously delicious. Maybe it was the chipotle mayo, but I thought they were addicting. Yum yum. I was not really a fan of the deconstructed huevos rancheros (why am I assembling my own food? So much work ahh) but I'd definitely come back to try their other foods.
