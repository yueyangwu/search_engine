      • Kim Sun Young Beauty Salon
        $$ Hair Salons, Nail Salons
        306 N Western Ave
        Los Angeles, CA 90004
        3/15/2015

        **FOR STRAIGHT PERM ONLY**

        I got my straightened by Cindy Chun and her assistant Alex, who were both very friendly and spoke decent English. The whole process took about 3.5 hours..

        It depends on your hair length, but it is about $250 for Japanese straightening, or $250 for keratin treatment (brings back the shine to hairs & lessens frizz but only lasts 2-3 month); or combination for $350. Unless you have very healthy hair, they'll recommend the keratin only or the combination. I opted for the combination and the results were stick straight hair. It turned out great, but I didn't like how flat my bangs looked, so I think next time, I will just get the keratin treatment or at least not have them treat my bangs.

        Cindy was awesome and even dyed my hair at no cost because she said my previous color (a sort of ashy dark brown) made my hair look more damaged.

        Parking: There are 2 lots-- the main lot and a backlot that you can enter off of Oxford.

      • Mizu 212
        $$$ Japanese
        2000 Sawtelle Blvd
        Los Angeles, CA 90025
        3/15/2015
        3 check-ins

        A little on the expensive side, but their meat is really good and I love not having to trek out to Little Tokyo for shabu.

        Their most basic order (USDA Prime Grade Rib-Eye- $23) is definitely sufficient in quality and amount. The order comes with kabocha, enoki mushrooms, napa cabbage, baby bok choi, green onions, broccoli, shiitake, tofu, and udon, as well as brown/white rice.

        Make sure to ask them to make you their special dipping sauce. Every seat is set with 2 dipping sauces-- sesame and ponzu, but the servers here will make you a special one that is ponzu-based with chili pepper, garlic, and yuzu kosho.

      • Blue Daisy
        $$ Breakfast & Brunch, American (New), Cocktail Bars
        609 Broadway
        Santa Monica, CA 90401
        3/15/2015
        2 check-ins

        Great brunch spot that has a solid mix of savory and sweet dishes. The sweet dishes are, in my mind, out of this world, but they are all practically desserts, so beware.

        Blue Daisy offers both indoor and outdoor seating, and the staff is always friendly. There can be a good 20-30 minute wait on the weekends, so plan accordingly.

        Here are a couple items that keep me coming back:
        - Lemon ricotta and lemon sauce crepe-- I am in love with these fluffy crepes. The lemon sauce is tart and not too sweet, and the sweet ricotta balances the tartness out nicely.

        - Turkey sandwich. Usually, I don't order turkey sandwiches because they never sound exciting to me, but this one was amazing. Grilled red onions, pickles, cucumber, lemon mayo-mustard spread on toasted sourdough. The best turkey sandwich ever.

      • Brian’s Shave Ice
        $ Shaved Ice, Bubble Tea
        11301 W Olympic Blvd
        Los Angeles, CA 90064
        12/20/2014
        3 check-ins

        More finely crushed shaved ice exists on this earth, but for the mainland, this is probably the finest shaved ice you'll get-- and it's delicious!!

        The fruity flavors are very sweet and syrupy, which can hit the spot if that's what you're craving, but my solid go-to flavors are green tea and coconut. Neither of them are too sweet and are complemented perfectly by a topping of rich, condensed milk.

        Check in on Yelp and you get a free filling, which can be ice cream, Dole Whip, mochi balls, or azuki beans. The black sesame ice cream wasn't too flavorful, but that might just be because my tongue was pretty numb from all the ice by the time I reached the ice cream...

      • Hong Kong Lounge
        $$ Dim Sum, Seafood, Cantonese
        5322 Geary Blvd
        San Francisco, CA 94121
        12/2/2014
        2 check-ins

        Delicious dim sum! There is usually a 20-30 minute wait on the weekend, but that's really not too bad considering how long waits can get at brunch these days.

        This place doesn't have carts, and you order off the menu by writing in what you want. I've never had any of their large plates, but there is such a large variety of dim sum dishes, and they are all really good. My favorites are the vegetarian spring rolls, which are fried just perfectly; the lotus leaf sticky rice; and the steamed pork buns.

      • Garza Blanca
        $$$ Hotels
        Carretera a Barra de Navidad KM 7.5
        48390 Puerto Vallarta, Jalisco
        Mexico
        12/2/2014

        My husband and I had a wonderful stay at the beautiful Garza Blanca. We arrived around midnight, so they had fewer staff to check us in, but they were friendly and courteous, and got us checked in as promptly as they could. Make sure to inquire about the activities of the week if you arrive after their activities desk closes-- the resort organizes complimentary yoga classes, hikes, runs, snorkeling, etc. We didn't end up going on any of their organized tours, but we did go on a little hike of our own to explore the preserve. On our hike, we ran into Tito, who leads many of these hikes, and he let us into a little swimming hole.

        Our room was in Tower 4, which is a 3 minute golf cart ride up the hill; but aside from the first trip up with our luggage, we walked to and from the lobby, which is really only a 5 minute walk because there is a shortcut. The view from these towers on the hill are spectacular, and our room was impeccable too, with a large balcony.

        If you are planning on going into town during your stay, the all-inclusive meal plan may not be worth it. We decided against it because we were spending 1 of 3 days in town, and we were right not to get it. It would be a good deal if you're a big eater & drinker, but we are neither. The restaurants are pretty good-- we enjoyed the breakfast (you can get it both in the restaurant by the lobby and at Boca Dos) and Boca Dos Stk in particular.

        The staff was very accommodating, which we appreciated. We had wanted to go snorkeling, but their beach was closed due to larger than usual waves (an effect from the hurricane that had hit Los Cabos days earlier). When we told them we were experienced snorkelers and really wanted some time in the ocean, they arranged for one of their staff to drive us about a half mile down the road to a beach where the waves weren't as big. Talk about great customer service!

        We went in September, during Puerto Vallarta's rainy season (which is July through September), and I would recommend going then for anyone looking for a good deal. Rooms were about half off, and we had no problems getting the poolside lounge chairs or a spot on the treadmill in the mornings.

        Though it's considered their rainy season, don't worry when you check the weather forecast and it says 80% rain for the entire duration of your stay (as it forecasted for mine..); close to the water, it really only rains in the evening. For us, at least, it only rained after sunset and through the night, and the staff all told us that was common for this time of year.
