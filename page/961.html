There is chocolate that looks like sliced cheese because life is wonderful

5.8k
Shares
Share
Tweet
Share
What's This?
By Andrea Romano2015-12-09 16:56:51 UTC

There's a brand new way to get more sweet, delicious chocolate into your diet.

Japanese company Bourbon, which specializes in chocolate and other confections, developed a super convenient sliced chocolate that resembles those Kraft American cheese slices your mom used to put on your grilled cheese sandwiches.

The chocolate slices are made of "nama chocolate," which is a rich and thick combination of cocoa and heavy cream, making it soft, pliable and more intense in flavor. You can use them for a variety of different dessert dishes.

For instance, you can melt them over toast, stuff them into crepes, cut them into shapes for hors d'oeuvres or roll them into cake rolls. Or you can just eat them by the slice. We won't judge you.

The chocolate is available on Bourbon's online store, but they're only sold in bulk. For 3,240 yen, about $27 USD, you can buy a dozen, five-pack slices.

Now we're all craving grilled chocolate sandwiches for lunch.
