      • Sam Wo Restaurant
        $ Chinese
        713 Clay St
        San Francisco, CA 94108
        10/19/2015
        1 check-in First to Review

        Sam Wo is back!  Imagine my surprise when I happened upon Sam Wo today while I was hunting for a place for lunch in Chinatown.  It turns out that today is their first day open after their old location closed over 3 years ago.  Brings back such fond memories!

        Don't expect rude staff, greasy food, or a grimy atmosphere.  The new Sam Wo is bright & clean with fresh, delicious food!  I have to admit I was kind of hoping to be berated by the little old waitress from years ago, but she was nowhere to be found.  I'll just have to remember the good ol' days when we would go to Sam Wo for cheap food, BYOB, dingy dining room, & ridiculously rude staff.  Until the bill came, then it was "Thank you very much!!" with a huge smile.  It turns out you can get all that at numerous restaurants in Chinatown, so go to the new Sam Wo for the friendly staff & yummy Chinese food.

      • Loard’s Ice Cream
        $ Ice Cream & Frozen Yogurt
        2151 Salvio St
        Concord, CA 94520
        7/22/2015

        I was very surprised to hear that Loard's had opened up in downtown Concord.  I honestly didn't even know they were still around.  When I heard they served ube ice cream, I just had to go!  Turns out they had just opened the day before so the place was sparkling clean.  The staff are all so nice & they give out as many samples as you like.  

        We decided on the banana & ube flavors.  We tried a few others (including one that was chocolate overload...was it Double Dutch?) but thought these were the best.  My only complaint is that the ice cream was semi-melted.  I'm not sure if that was because it was a really hot/humid day or if it was because they just opened.  It just made for a very melty ice cream cone.  I ate the whole thing anyway & thoroughly enjoyed each bite!

        Loard's is a great addition to Todos Santos Square!

      • Pro Car Wash
        Auto Detailing, Car Wash
        2300 Monument Blvd.
        Pleasant Hill, CA 94523
        6/8/2015

        After missing my usual car wash spot by mere minutes (they close at 5, I arrived at 5:03), I was in a panic to find a car wash open past 5 on a Saturday.  Is that too much to ask?  In the Concord area, apparently so.  Now I am not picky when it comes to car washes.  Really, I could care less about how my vehicle looks.  All I wanted that day was for someone to clean our truck well enough so I could throw a futon in the back & go to the drive in.  According to Yelp this place was the only car wash open until 6 so I didn't have much choice.

        This is essentially a car wash hut attached to a gas station, but the gas station is not affiliated with the car wash.  I tried to pay inside the gas station & the guy said to just get in line & pay at the car wash.  I got in line & immediately regretted it, but I was stuck since someone drove in right behind me so I was trapped.  There are no prices or services listed.  I had to laugh when I got up closer to the little hut & realized that it's not even a car wash, it's a guy with a hose & a sponge!  All the car wash electronics & dancing drapes are gone, it's just one guy soaping up your car.  I cringed at the thought of all that water going down the drain & not being recycled.  Well there was no turning back now so I just sat & waited for my truck to be washed.  

        When he's done you drive up to the "drying station".  Another LOL moment happened when the little old lady hustled up with a shop vac & an extra long extension cord that was dragging through standing water.  While she vacuums your car, a few other guys dry the outside & wipe down the windows.  I had another moment of panic when I realized that there are no credit card machines here & no prices listed, just a guy with a wad of cash that throws out a price.  Did I even have cash?  What if I didn't?  Luckily I had just enough to cover the wash & give a couple bucks for a tip.  I have to say that they did work pretty hard & did a decent job.  But this place is just too weird for me to ever come back.

      • Federalist Public House & Beer Garden
        $$ Pizza, Beer Gardens
        2009 Matsui Aly
        Sacramento, CA 95811
        3/12/2015
        2 check-ins
        Listed in Beeeeeeer!

        Great vibes at this cool little spot in downtown Sacramento.  The place is made out of old shipping containers, which sounds weird & boring but they did an amazing job making it feel homey & modern.  It appears to be completely open on one side so I wonder if it ever gets too cold or hot in there?  Fortunately we were there on a beautiful spring day so the temperature was perfect.

        The staff are friendly & funny.  We were there at the end of Sac Beer Week & they had a tap takeover from Berryessa Brewing.  I tried their pale ale (Marley something, I think?) & it was very light & refreshing.  They also have a pizza oven in back (I can only guess how hot it must be near that oven during the Sacramento summers).  Everyone was eating pizza & drinking beer just having a good ol' time.  Some folks were lounging in the sun on Adirondack chairs.

        As if everyone wasn't already having a good time, the staff started up a bingo game.  The prizes were t-shirts & discounted food & drinks.  Who knew bingo could be so fun?  Everyone got into it & a hush went over the entire place.  When someone yelled "Bingo!" everyone clapped & congratulated the winner.  They paced out the games about every half hour or so, which gave people time to order up more food & beer & wait for the next game.  I could have stayed there all day drinking beer, playing bingo, & making new friends!  Alas, the other breweries in Sacramento were calling so we had to move on.

        I will definitely be back to The Federalist next time I'm in Sacramento!

      • Briones Regional Park
        Parks, Hiking
        2537 Reliez Valley Rd
        Martinez, CA 94553
        1/9/2015
        3 check-ins

        This has become my favorite East Bay park close to my house.  Whenever I feel like I want to do a butt-kicker hike but only have a couple of hours, I usually go to Briones.  Whether you start on the PH/Martinez, Lafayette, or Orinda sides you will gain some serious elevation (about 1,400 feet) & get quite a workout.  And the view at the top is amazing!

        I usually start at the Alhambra Creek Staging Area in Martinez.  The Alhambra Creek trail is less steep than others & still gets you to the top of Briones peak.  From the Reliez Valley Staging Area, the hike up is shorter but much steeper (especially the last 1/2 mile before the peak).  Once you are at the top you have an amazing unobstructed view.  To the east you can see Mt. Diablo & the Diablo Valley, to the south you can see Las Trampas & the Tri Valley, to the west you can see the Oakland/Berkeley hills & Mount Tam, and to the north you can see the Carquinez Strait.  There's even a nice bench at the top where you can rest your weary legs (you'll need it!).  

        Go out & explore our incredible East Bay Parks!

      • Opa! Authentic Greek Cuisine
        $$ Greek
        1532 N Main St
        Walnut Creek, CA 94596
        11/3/2014
        2 check-ins
        Listed in BARTable

        After eating here on Friday night for my hubby's birthday, he declared "I'd give it 2 stars".  Since he's not a Yelper I decided to give it my own rating of 3 stars since I liked about half of the dishes & our waitress was nice.  But don't let the name of the restaurant fool you...Opa! Authentic Greek Cuisine is not very authentic.

        We started with the drink menu.  Luckily they did have Retsina (Greek wine with a strong pine resin taste), otherwise I'm sure we would have walked.  I was pleased to see Rombauer chardonnay on the menu which is my absolute favorite so I got myself a glass & let hubby enjoy his Retsina.  It is definitely an acquired taste.  

        The appetizer menu itself is pretty huge so we ordered a few to start.  The saganaki (flaming kasseri cheese) was the big winner.  They yell "Opa!" when they light it on fire which always makes hubby cringe, but we Americans seem to love that part.  We also tried 3 dips with pitas.  The pita bread here is so think & dense & although it is delicious, it will fill you up very quickly.  The tiny portions of dips were almost laughable.  For $16 you get about a tablespoonful of each dip & a pile of pita bread.  The skordalia was delicious (watch out for garlic breath for the next 8 hours), tzatziki was good, eggplant dip was decent.  We also ordered the dolmas which are definitely made in house, yet they were pretty bland.  It was basically rice pilaf rolled in grape leaves.  Meh.

        I'm glad we held off on ordering our main dishes cuz I was so stuffed after eating the apps.  I was really looking forward to the grilled whole sea bass, which is my most favorite Greek dish of all time.  But I was so stuffed from the appetizers that I decided not to order a main dish at all.  I'm pretty sure this was due to the dense pita bread, which we only ate about half of.  Note to Opa:  cool it on the pita bread offerings!  Hubby decided against the lamb souvlaki & ordered one more appetizer, the Greek sausage.  There was nothing Greek about it, other than the olive oil & lemon juice drizzled on top.  It was just your basic run of the mill pork sausage.  Disappointing.  

        Needless to say I still owe my hubby a real authentic Greek dinner at Kokkari.

      • Heart of the City Farmers Market
        $ Fruits & Veggies, Farmers Market
        1182 Market St
        San Francisco, CA 94102
        10/23/2014
        12 check-ins

        My new fave farmer's market!

        Since my local FM ended this month & won't start up again until May, I needed to find another place to buy my produce.  The Ferry Building is OK but always so crowded, and the Crocker Galleria is small & cramped.  Head out to the Civic Center & you will find a sprawling farmer's market larger than any other downtown weekday market!

        Everything here looks beautiful & the colors of the season are on full display.  The vendors are always friendly & more than happy to give you samples of their goods.  There's one booth that sells mostly greens, and you will find the freshest lettuce, kale, parsley, cilantro, pennywort, & bittermelon leaves around (I only know those last 2 thanks to my Burmese coworkers).  I also love the vendor that sells 10 different varieties of apples as well as a huge spread of various plants, all for bargain prices.  Oh and the almond guys?  Don't even get started on those things cuz you will get addicted like me.  Butter toffee, maple, orange, applewood smoked, jalapeno...they are all delicious!

        There are also a few food vendors (the tamales are a big hit) & lots of outdoor tables to enjoy your lunch.  Sure it's not in the best neighborhood, but there are plenty of heavily armed police making their presence known.  They almost look like SWAT members.  They do their job too...last week I saw them take down a guy who got into a heated argument with a customer.

        This certainly isn't the most convenient location for me, but the great selection & amazing quality of the produce keeps me coming back.

      • Xi’an Famous Foods
        $ Chinese, Burgers, Sandwiches
        24 W 45th St
        New York, NY 10036
        10/8/2014
        1 check-in

        I have been dreaming about their spicy stewed pork noodles ever since I left New York.  Surely with all these great Chinese restaurants in SF I should be able to find something close to what Xi'an dishes out?  Not so.  I guess I will have to keep dreaming.

        As everyone else mentions, the place is small & gets crowded so best to come early.  Fortunately for me they were located very close to my hotel so I got there just after noon on a Saturday.  The line wasn't too long but all of the chairs were taken so I had to stand & slurp my noodles.  I didn't care.  I was shoveling those deliciously wide, spicy noodles into my mouth so fast that I didn't even want to stop long enough to take a photo.  I managed to take one photo then continued on with my slurping, getting bright red spicy sauce all over my phone & my white t-shirt.

        I guess this place has been featured on some Food Network shows & is frequented by celebrity chefs (just look at all the pics on the wall), in case you care about that kind of stuff.  All I wanted was some good spicy Chinese noodles & Xi'an came through for me!
